# World Premise — Brave Bunny

> One page. The world, the stakes, the heroes, the tone. Owner: narrative-designer. Consumers: art-director, level-designer, ui-engineer, localizer.

## The Brave Bunny Burrow

Tucked under a sunny meadow sits the **Brave Bunny Burrow**, a cozy underground network of warm tunnels, soft lamps, and a very large kitchen. This is where the animal heroes sleep, train, and share supper between adventures. Above ground, the world is bright and wide. Below ground, it smells like fresh hay and warm bread.

## The Wild Outside

Past the Burrow's front door stretches the **Wild Outside** — five sunny regions where the heroes go on their runs.

- **Meadow** — tall grass, daisies, and bouncy **rascals**.
- **Beach** — warm sand, tide pools, and splashy little **scamps**.
- **Forest** — friendly shade, mossy stones, and quick **troublemakers**.
- **Cavern** — soft glowstones, echoing tunnels, and curious **wee beasties**.
- **Snow** — round drifts, pine trees, and chilly **snow-pests**.

Each region has its own little nuisance, and every so often a much bigger one shows up — a **Big Trouble**. Big Troubles are not evil. They are just having a rough day.

## The Reason

The rascals have a problem: they keep eating all the carrots. Without carrots, the Burrow cannot bake its **weekly carrot-cake feast**. The whole place would just have toast. Toast is fine. But it is not cake.

So the heroes hop out, biff a few scamps, and bring the carrots home. The stakes are small. The cake is real.

## The Heroes

It starts with **Bunny**, who never could sit still when there was hopping to be done. Word spreads through the burrow, and friends start showing up. A sturdy **Tortoise** with a kind shell. A swift **Fox** with a sharp grin. A bristly **Hedgehog**, a splashy **Otter**, a sleepy **Panda**, a busy **Badger**, and a watchful **Owl**.

Each one joins for their own soft reason: a cousin to visit, a hobby to try, a new friend to meet. Nobody is running from anything. Everybody is running toward cake.

## The Tone

The Burrow is never lonely. The Wild Outside is never grim. Heroes who run out of pep are simply tuckered out — a quick nap, a warm drink, and they are ready again. The Big Troubles are misunderstood, not malicious. **Old Boar King** is not cruel; he is just sleepy and grumpy and stepped on the wrong daisy. **Big Owl** is not menacing; she is just very particular about bedtime.

Nothing here bites. Nothing here bleeds. Everything here is fond.

---

## Why this premise

This world supports our positioning on three fronts. First, **no-pay-to-win** reads naturally in a place where the prize is carrot cake — there is no whale fantasy to sell, no power gap to dramatize, no FOMO to manufacture. Carrots are carrots. Second, **family-safe** is built in at the fiction layer, not bolted on at the rating layer. A grandparent reading over a child's shoulder in Istanbul, Manila, or Jakarta should smile, not flinch. No skulls, no shouting, no edge.

Third, the **TR / PH / ID** soft-launch markets share a cultural soft spot for warm, communal food-and-family stories. A weekly feast that everyone bakes together translates cleanly across all three regions, far better than a chosen-one prophecy or a revenge plot would. The carrot cake is the brand. The Burrow is the home base. The Wild Outside is the playground. That is the whole game in one paragraph, and it travels.

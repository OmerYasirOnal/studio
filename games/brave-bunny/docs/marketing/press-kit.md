## Description

### Short (140 chars)

A fearless rabbit, ten minutes of low-poly chaos, and the cleanest roguelite math on mobile. No pay-to-win, no energy gates, just one more run.

### Medium (60 words)

Brave Bunny is a saturated low-poly action-roguelite for iOS and Android. Pick a weapon, hop into a sun-soaked meadow, survive ten minutes against waves of foxes, otters and boss bees. Hand-tuned weapon evolutions and a TTK-balanced difficulty curve make every run feel fresh. Free to play, no pay-to-win, no energy gates, no gear gacha.

### Long (180 words)

In **Brave Bunny**, you are a fearless rabbit knight with a tiny sword and big problems. Hordes of foxes pour into a meadow of glossy carrots and sun-baked grass; otters charge from the riverbank; bee-bosses arrive every two minutes carrying telegraphed patterns you must dance through. You have ten minutes to survive, and ten minutes is the only commitment Brave Bunny asks of you.

Every weapon has a charm-fused evolution form, tuned on a TTK ladder rather than vibes. The art is saturated low-poly cartoon — a meadow you want to live in — running at a locked 60 fps on a five-year-old phone. Audio is warm and chunky, not chiptune.

Brave Bunny ships with a public design pledge: **no pay-to-win, no energy gates, no gear gacha, no SKU above $19.99**. All purchases are cosmetic or convenience. The game is offline-first, ships with no third-party analytics SDK, and respects your data: telemetry is local and opt-in to share.

Built solo, with the help of a Claude-Code agent swarm coordinated through git.

## What makes it different

- **TTK ladder as design instrument.** Every weapon, every wave, every boss is tuned against a measured time-to-kill curve. Not vibes.
- **The decline button is always the larger button.** On every opt-in IAP and rewarded-ad surface, "No thanks" is 120 pt and pre-focused; "Yes" is 88 pt. This is in our positioning doc and verified in every UI review.
- **Privacy is a feature, not a footnote.** Built without Firebase, GameAnalytics, Adjust, or any attribution SDK. Telemetry lives on your device unless you choose to email it to us.
- **Built in eight weeks by one person.** The dev pipeline is its own story — a swarm of Claude-Code agents coordinated through filesystem and git. Happy to talk to anyone who wants to dig into solo-dev tooling.
